SIMPLE QUERIES (1-5)
Direct fact lookups from the knowledge base
Query 1
Natural Language: Did Alice purchase a laptop?
 Prolog: purchased(alice, laptop).
 Expected: true
Query 2
Natural Language: Is Bob a gold loyalty member?
 Prolog: loyalty_member(bob, gold).
 Expected: false
Query 3
Natural Language: What category is a smartphone in?
 Prolog: category(smartphone, X).
 Expected: X = electronics
Query 4
Natural Language: Is the tablet in stock?
 Prolog: in_stock(tablet).
 Expected: true
Query 5
Natural Language: How much does a laptop cost?
 Prolog: price(laptop, X).
 Expected: X = 1200

MULTI-STEP QUERIES (11-15)
Requires multiple rule applications and complex logic
Query 11
Natural Language: Which customers are both high-value and high spenders?
 Prolog: high_value_customer(X), high_spender(X).
 Expected: X = alice; X = carol
Query 12
Natural Language: What electronics products did gold members purchase?
 Prolog: loyalty_member(X, gold), purchased(X, Product), category(Product, electronics).
 Expected: X = alice, Product = laptop; X = carol, Product = tablet
Query 13
Natural Language: Which premium products are currently out of stock?
 Prolog: premium_product(X), out_of_stock(X).
 Expected: X = smartwatch
Query 14
Natural Language: How many products has each customer purchased?
 Prolog: findall(P, purchased(Customer, P), Products), length(Products, Count).
 Expected: alice = 3, bob = 2, carol = 3, david = 2, emily = 1, frank = 2
Query 15
Natural Language: Which customers bought products in the same month as Alice?
 Prolog: purchased_month(alice, _, Month), purchased_month(X, _, Month), X \= alice.
 Expected: X = bob (february)
